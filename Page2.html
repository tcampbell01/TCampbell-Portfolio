Bio: Teresa Campbell is a software engineer, instructional designer, educator, and musician. She is currently an engineer at Optum where she works on a team in the provider search and directory space. Most recently her team built and put into production over 100 data movement and storage components, using translators, foundation blocks, and factories to move data from on-prem to AWS. In addition to software engineering, Teresa has extensive experience as an educator. She has taught children and adults in the areas of violin, viola, orchestra, English as a Second Language, GED coaching, and citizenship preparation. She holds a Bachelor of Music in Violin Performance, a Master of Music in Music Education, a second Master of Arts in English as a Second Language, and a certificate in Web Development from the University of Connecticut. Outside of her work at Optum, Teresa designs asynchronous online professional development courses for educators, maintains a small studio of violin and viola students, and is a substitute violinist with the Hartford Symphony, Springfield Symphony, and New Haven Symphony. She currently sits on the Board of Directors for both American Mensa and the Mensa Foundation.
